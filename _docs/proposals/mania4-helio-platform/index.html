Executive Summary

This is not only a website. It is a commerce operating platform that lets Mania4 launch branded stores, sell personalized products, and control payment, production, pickup, delivery, and reconciliation from one reusable system.

The proposed platform turns HELIOtextil's existing strengths in personalization, sports equipment, and promotional materials into a scalable digital product. Mania4 becomes the owned retail and payment layer. HELIOPromo and Identity FC become flagship use cases. Clubs, universities, festivals, and event partners can then be launched as replicated storefronts with their own products, domains, QR codes, MUPI/tablet flows, and fulfillment rules.

Client Context

HELIOtextil Group

Industrial credibility

HELIOtextil has decades of textile production expertise across labels, transfers, emblems, personalized products, sports, technical textiles, fashion, packaging, and promotional sectors.

HELIOPromo

Next generation merchandise

HELIOPromo is positioned around original, certified, sustainable merchandise and event activation. The platform should make this offer easier to sell, replicate, and measure.

Identity FC

Football and sport focus

Identity FC can use the same platform for football clubs, member discounts, team stores, sponsor logos, player names, numbers, pickup points, and repeat orders.

Business Opportunity

Mania4 can own the transaction while every partner gets a branded buying experience.

The operating model discussed in the meeting is clear: Mania4 controls checkout, payment, invoicing, and order data. The partner store, club, or event receives orders to produce or hand over, then reconciliation happens through reports, redemption codes, or agreed settlement rules.

Key principle: replicate the Shopify-like convenience of fast store creation, but with Mania4 owning the commerce rules, checkout, personalization engine, and production assets.

Scope of Work

The SoW is structured as six workstreams. They can be presented as two commercial rubrics for funding and procurement, while remaining one coherent platform implementation.

Institutional commerce presence

  • Mania4 / HELIOPromo storefront foundation.
  • Content pages for brand story, sustainability, services, and campaigns.
  • Product catalog, categories, product detail pages, cart, checkout handoff, and confirmation.
  • Clear brand experience for B2C and B2B audiences.
  • Baseline store that becomes the reference for future client stores.

Client and event store replication

  • Admin flow to create stores from a template.
  • Per-store logo, colors, content, product selection, domain, QR code, delivery options, and access rules.
  • Support for clubs, festivals, universities, physical shops, and event booths.
  • Example: Nova activation, NOS Alive, Rock in Rio, football club store, or university merchandise shop.
  • Store launch becomes configuration work instead of a new build each time.

3D customization and print-on-demand

  • Embed the 3D customization engine into product pages.
  • Product configuration schema for model, texture, zones, colors, fonts, constraints, and pricing modifiers.
  • Customer inputs for base color, text, player name, number, logo upload, and preview.
  • Export design ID, preview, print texture, and structured configuration JSON.
  • Supports apparel and promotional materials where a product model or validated template exists.
  • Automatic 3D creation from arbitrary photos is treated as R&D, not guaranteed MVP scope.

Order operations and fulfillment

  • Order back office for Mania4 and optional partner/operator roles.
  • Production states: paid, pending production, in production, ready for pickup, shipped, delivered, cancelled.
  • Pickup validation by code, QR, or operator view.
  • Delivery and in-store pickup options per store or activation.
  • Works for online delivery, club shop pickup, and event booth handover.
  • Partner does not need to run payment locally to fulfill the order.

Payments, invoicing and settlement

  • Centralized Mania4 checkout and payment configuration.
  • Payment methods aligned with the selected provider, including future physical terminal/MUPI flow where supported.
  • Partner reporting for redemptions, products sold, quantities, taxes, fees, and monthly settlement.
  • Mania4 controls the payment relationship and banking negotiation.
  • Partner reconciliation can be report-based before deeper ERP/API integrations.

Integrations and data imports

  • Member discount imports through CSV, Excel mapping, SFTP, or API where available.
  • API hooks for partner order notification where needed.
  • Storage for 3D assets, generated previews, print files, and configuration history.
  • Future event stream for production telemetry and IoT machine counters.
  • Start with robust imports and notifications; expand to direct APIs only when partner systems justify it.
  • IoT and printer telemetry are recommended as a later phase once the commerce flow is stable.

Technical Architecture

A single API-first platform powers all storefronts, checkout, customization, order operations, and future integrations.

The architecture builds on an existing multi-tenant commerce foundation: Next.js, React, Neon Postgres, Drizzle ORM, tenant data isolation, hosted checkout, payment provider adapters, object storage, and typed APIs. The 3D customization engine adds React Three Fiber, a deterministic texture pipeline, a zone authoring studio, and production export endpoints.

How the pieces fit together — from sales channels down to infrastructure. The Commerce API is the single contract every app, partner, and AI agent connects through.

Deliverables and Acceptance Criteria

Platform deliverables
  • Reusable storefront template with Mania4/HELIOPromo reference implementation.
  • Multi-store admin setup flow for stores, products, branding, domains, QR links, and fulfillment options.
  • Central checkout flow under Mania4 payment ownership.
  • Back office for orders, production status, pickup validation, and settlement reporting.
  • Technical documentation for environments, store creation, operations, and launch process.
3D and POD deliverables
  • Customer-facing 3D configurator embedded on selected product detail pages.
  • Admin/designer zone studio for product personalization areas.
  • Validated product configuration schema for model, texture, zones, colors, fonts, constraints, and pricing modifiers.
  • Generated preview image, design ID, configuration JSON, and print/export asset per confirmed design.
  • Production handoff visible inside the order detail.
Acceptance criteria
  • A customer can enter a store, select a product, customize where applicable, pay through Mania4 checkout, and receive confirmation.
  • An operator can view the order, access design/production files, update status, and mark pickup or delivery complete.
  • A new partner/event store can be configured without code changes for standard template use cases.
  • Member/import discounts can be tested through the agreed CSV/SFTP/API approach.
  • The site works on desktop, tablet, and mobile, with a kiosk-safe path for MUPI/tablet activations.
Quality criteria
  • Core user journeys pass functional QA before launch.
  • Checkout and order state changes are auditable.
  • Uploaded artwork is validated for allowed file types and size limits.
  • Production secrets and payment credentials are not exposed to storefront users.
  • Launch documentation is complete enough for Mania4 operators to run the pilot.

Assumptions and Dependencies

Content and brand

HELIOtextil provides final copy, logos, campaign images, legal content, product descriptions, pricing rules, and approval for the client-facing naming system.

Products and 3D assets

The MVP uses selected pilot products with validated 3D models, UV layouts, or agreed 2D/3D templates. New product modeling and automatic photo-to-3D workflows are estimated separately.

Payments and operations

Mania4 confirms payment provider, invoicing responsibility, tax handling, refund rules, pickup/delivery procedures, and settlement model with partners before launch.

Partner systems

Member discounts and partner order notifications depend on the data each club, university, or event partner can provide: API, CSV, Excel, SFTP, or manual import.

Hardware

MUPI, payment terminal, printer, press, camera, NFC, QR reader, and thermal printer procurement or certification are outside the software build unless added as a hardware workstream.

Launch scope

The first release should focus on one reference store, a small set of products, one replicated activation, and a controlled order operations model before scaling the network.

Out of Scope Unless Separately Agreed

  • Replacing HELIOtextil's ERP, accounting, warehouse, or manufacturing systems.
  • Guaranteeing automatic high-quality 3D model creation from arbitrary customer photos.
  • Deep integration with every partner checkout or payment provider.
  • Hardware procurement, device certification, and on-site installation.
  • Full IoT machine telemetry, printer history extraction, or press counter reconciliation in MVP.
  • Native iOS/Android apps. The initial recommendation is a responsive web app and kiosk/tablet mode.
  • Unlimited product modeling. Each new product family requires an agreed asset and configuration process.

Commercial and Governance Notes

Recommended budget split

Two rubrics, one platform

To support the internationalization funding structure, the work can be described as two investment lines: institutional digital commerce presence and replicated online sales platform for client projects. The implementation should remain one shared platform to avoid duplicate systems.

Ownership model

Mania4 owns the software layer

Mania4 should own the application, database, configurations, and operating process. Managed cloud services can be used, but the platform should avoid lock-in to a third-party commerce SaaS as the core business engine.

Pilot governance

Start with controlled pilots

Recommended pilots are one HELIOPromo/Mania4 store, one football or university store, and one event/MUPI activation. Each pilot should have measurable conversion, production, pickup, and reconciliation criteria.

Future scale

Build toward a productized service

After MVP, Mania4 can sell this as a service: branded stores, custom merchandise, event activation, 3D design, production handoff, and settlement reporting for multiple markets.
